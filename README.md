This docs analyzes the relationship between traditional knowledge, environmental conservation, and sustainable tourism from the ethno-ornithological perspective of tour guides in the Parnaíba Delta Environmental Protection Area. By integrating semi-structured interviews, birdlife surveys, and spatial analyses, the work contributes to understanding the interactions between society and nature in a sustainable use Conservation Unit within the category of Environmental Protection Area, in line with the scope of the journal Sociedade & Natureza. Because the research involved human subjects, it was submitted to the Ethics Committee of the Federal University of Piauí, obtaining a favorable opinion under protocol number: 82323524.6.0000.5214 and opinion number: 7.339.335. This is an original research project, with an empirical approach that is still little explored in the field, highlighting the role of local knowledge in strengthening birdwatching and eco-development. The manuscript is unpublished and has not been submitted to any other journal.
